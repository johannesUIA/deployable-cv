ExitNode – Crisis Management Platform

Bachelor project · Spring 2025 · in collaboration with CIEM (UiA)

ExitNode was our Spring 2025 bachelor project, developed with CIEM (Centre for Integrated Emergency Management) at the University of Agder. We built a digital solution that registers, tracks, and shares information about evacuees during crises — a modern alternative to pen and paper.

The goal was to give reception centers a structured digital intake the moment people arrive, and to keep municipal crisis management updated on status, location, and reunifications in real time.

Technically, the solution is a Python/Flask web app with Azure SQL providing secure storage for personal and incident data. We also integrated IoT devices (Raspberry Pi/Arduino) to show how field sensors can report status directly.

The interplay between the web application, database, and IoT streams lets preparedness teams filter, search, and follow events in real time — even across multiple sites.

My responsibilities covered backend APIs, database schema, dashboards, and Docker deployment (Azure Web Apps + Container Registry). I designed the data model so evacuees, relatives, and incidents could be linked and updated without conflicts.

The result was faster registration, fewer manual errors, and far better decision support for crisis leadership — backed by clear overviews and searchable logs.
